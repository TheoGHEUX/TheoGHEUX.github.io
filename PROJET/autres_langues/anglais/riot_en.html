  • Riot Games is an American video game development and publishing company, founded in 2006. It is primarily known for its iconic game League of Legends, one of the most popular titles in the world of competitive online gaming. Riot Games is distinguished by its ability to create immersive gaming experiences, often focused on competition and eSports. The company has expanded its portfolio with other successful games like Valorant and Teamfight Tactics. Riot Games is also involved in producing content related to its games, including series and live events.

The founders of Riot Games

  • Brandon Beck and Marc Merrill are the co-founders of Riot Games. Longtime friends, they shared a passion for video games and envisioned a unique approach: developing high-quality competitive multiplayer games while adopting a "free-to-play" business model, which was still rare at the time. Their vision came to life with the launch of League of Legends in 2009, which quickly became one of the most popular games in the world, bringing together millions of players and spectators. Under their leadership, Riot Games also played a major role in the rise of esports, organizing international competitions followed by millions of fans. In 2017, Beck and Merrill decided to step down from daily operations to focus on creative projects and the development of new games, while remaining deeply involved in the long-term vision of Riot Games.

  • Riot Games' role in Arcane

    Riot Games played a central role in the creation of Arcane, an animated adaptation of the League of Legends universe. In collaboration with the French animation studio Fortiche Production, Riot Games oversaw every step of the creative process to ensure the series remained true to the identity and depth of its universe. The goal was to bring the stories and iconic characters of the game to life, while exploring universal themes such as power struggles, family bonds, and moral dilemmas. Riot Games also contributed its narrative expertise by enriching the character arcs and developing the complex relationships between them, particularly those of Jinx, Vi, and Jayce. By investing directly in the production rather than licensing its rights to a third party, Riot ensured that Arcane stayed true to its artistic vision and lore*, while providing an immersive and accessible experience, even for those unfamiliar with the game. This attention to detail made Arcane both a critical and popular success, further strengthening Riot Games' influence beyond the video game industry.

    Lore*: refers to the collection of stories, narratives, myths, and narrative elements that make up the fictional universe of a work.
